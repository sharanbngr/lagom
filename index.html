About Me

I am a doctoral candidate in physics at the University of Minnesota working with Prof. Vuk Mandic. I study gravitational-waves and their detection with ground-based and space-borne gravitational-wave detectors. My research focuses on detection and characterization of stochastic gravitational-wave backgrounds and transient gravitational-waves. I am a member of the LIGO scientific collaboration and the LISA consortium.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in gravitational-wave physics and data analysis methods extracting astrophysical and cosmological information from them. I am also particularly interested in studying binary neutron star merger remnants and have been involved in both gravitational-wave searches for these remnants and in development of statistical techniques to find and characterize them. Some of these techniques are also useful for searches targeting unmodeled or poorly modeled gravitational-wave sources.

On the other end of the spectrum, I also work on methods and techniques for detecting stochastic gravitational-wave backgrounds. These backgrounds can come from a variety of sources - with perhaps the most anticipated one being a hypothetical cosmological inflationary background.
